Module 1.2: Project Management for Instructional Design

What is Project Management

Project management is the systematic approach to planning, organizing, and controlling resources to achieve specific goals within defined constraints of time, budget, and scope (Project Management Institute, 2021). At its core, project management transforms ideas into reality through structured processes that ensure deliverables meet quality standards while staying on schedule and within budget.

For instructional designers, project management becomes the backbone that supports the complex process of creating effective learning experiences. It provides the framework needed to navigate the multifaceted challenges of developing educational content, coordinating with subject matter experts, managing stakeholder expectations, and delivering courses that truly meet learners' needs (Greer & Lin, 2021).

Why Project Management Matters in Instructional Design

Instructional design projects are inherently complex, involving multiple stakeholders, iterative design processes, and the challenge of translating learning objectives into engaging, effective educational experiences. Research indicates that instructional design projects without proper project management often suffer from scope creep, missed deadlines, budget overruns, and deliverables that fail to meet expectations (Allen & Gardner, 2014).

Project management brings discipline and structure to the creative process of instructional design. It ensures that creativity and pedagogical expertise are channeled effectively toward achieving specific learning outcomes within realistic constraints.
