Module 1.4: Project Management for Instructional Design

Applying Project Management to Online Course Development

Phase 1: Project Initiation and Analysis

This phase involves defining the project charter, identifying stakeholders, and conducting thorough needs analysis. Project management practices ensure that this foundational work is comprehensive and well-documented, providing a solid basis for all subsequent work (Branch, 2009).

Key activities include stakeholder mapping, learner analysis, content analysis, technical requirements gathering, and establishment of success metrics. The project manager works with instructional designers to ensure that all necessary information is collected and that project constraints are clearly understood by all parties (Allen & Gardner, 2014).

Phase 2: Design and Planning

During the design phase, instructional designers create detailed specifications for the course while project managers develop comprehensive project plans. This includes creating detailed work breakdown structures, establishing realistic timelines, allocating resources, and developing quality assurance procedures (Project Management Institute, 2021).

The design phase also involves creating prototypes and obtaining stakeholder approval for the overall approach before moving into full development. Project management ensures that this approval process is systematic and that all necessary sign-offs are obtained before proceeding (eLearning Industry, 2023).

Phase 3: Development and Production

The development phase is where content is created, multimedia elements are produced, and the course is built in the chosen delivery platform. Project management becomes crucial here for coordinating the work of multiple team members, managing dependencies between different work streams, and ensuring that quality standards are maintained throughout the production process (Greer & Lin, 2021).

Regular progress reviews, quality checkpoints, and stakeholder feedback sessions help keep the project on track and ensure that any issues are identified and addressed quickly. Studies show that structured development processes can reduce rework by up to 60% in instructional design projects (WeLearn Learning Services, 2024).

Phase 4: Implementation and Launch

Implementation involves deploying the course to the production environment, conducting final testing, and launching the course to learners. Project management ensures that this process is systematic and that all necessary preparations are completed before launch (Branch, 2009).

This phase also includes preparing support materials, training administrators and instructors, and establishing processes for ongoing support and maintenance. Effective implementation planning can reduce post-launch issues by up to 70% (Allen & Gardner, 2014).

Phase 5: Evaluation and Closure

The evaluation phase involves collecting and analyzing data about the course's effectiveness and the project's success. Project management practices ensure that this evaluation is systematic and that lessons learned are documented for future projects (Project Management Institute, 2021).

Project closure involves completing all deliverables, conducting project retrospectives, and transitioning any ongoing responsibilities to appropriate team members. Research indicates that systematic project closure processes improve knowledge retention and facilitate continuous improvement in instructional design organizations (eLearning Industry, 2023).
