Module 1.6: Project Management for Instructional Design

Wrapping Up

Project management serves as the essential foundation that transforms instructional design from a creative endeavor into a systematic, results-driven practice. By integrating structured project management principles with instructional design expertise, designers can navigate the complex challenges of educational content development while consistently delivering high-quality learning experiences within established constraints.

The evidence presented throughout this module demonstrates that project management doesn't constrain creativity—it enables it by providing clear frameworks for scope definition, timeline management, resource allocation, and stakeholder communication. Research consistently shows that instructional design projects utilizing formal project management practices achieve 35% higher success rates and are significantly more likely to be completed on time and within budget.

The systematic approach outlined in this module—from project initiation through evaluation and closure—provides instructional designers with the tools needed to manage the inherent complexity of educational content development. The frameworks and practices introduced here form the foundation for the more detailed project management skills explored in subsequent modules, ultimately enabling instructional designers to create more effective learning experiences while building stronger professional credibility within their organizations.
