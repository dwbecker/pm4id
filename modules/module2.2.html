Understanding Project Scope in Instructional Design

What is a Project Scope Statement?

A project scope statement is a comprehensive document that outlines the project's deliverables, objectives, boundaries, and constraints (Kerzner, 2017). In instructional design, this document serves as the contract between the design team and stakeholders, clearly defining what will and will not be included in the final learning solution. The scope of an Instructional Design project defines the boundaries and deliverables of the project. It encompasses the desired learning outcomes, content coverage, instructional strategies, assessment methods, and overall project objectives (eLearning Industry, 2023).

The scope statement acts as a critical reference point throughout the project lifecycle, helping to prevent scope creepâ€”one of the most common causes of project delays and budget overruns in instructional design projects (Allen & Gardner, 2014). Research indicates that projects with clearly defined scope statements experience 40% fewer change requests and are completed 25% faster than projects without such documentation (Dick et al., 2015).
