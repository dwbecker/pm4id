Module 2.3: Developing a Timeline and Project Scope Statement

Key Components of an Instructional Design Project Scope Statement

Project Objectives and Learning Outcomes

The scope statement should begin with a clear articulation of the project's educational objectives and specific learning outcomes. This section should include measurable learning objectives written using action verbs that describe what learners will be able to do upon completion of the course. These objectives should align with organizational goals and address identified performance gaps or learning needs (Morrison et al., 2019).

For online course development, objectives should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART). They should also consider the unique characteristics of online learning environments, including the need for self-directed learning capabilities and digital literacy skills (Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001).

Target Audience Analysis

A comprehensive scope statement must include detailed information about the target audience, including demographics, prior knowledge levels, learning preferences, technology access, and any special considerations such as accessibility requirements or cultural factors. This analysis directly impacts design decisions and helps establish realistic expectations for project complexity and resource requirements (Gagné et al., 2005).

Understanding the target audience also influences the selection of instructional strategies, media formats, and delivery methods. For example, a course designed for experienced professionals will have different scope requirements than one designed for entry-level learners (Clark & Mayer, 2016).

Content Scope and Coverage

This section defines the specific content areas that will be covered in the course, including the depth and breadth of coverage for each topic. It should clearly articulate what content will be included and, equally importantly, what will be excluded from the project scope. This helps prevent scope creep and ensures that all stakeholders have aligned expectations about the final deliverable (Kerzner, 2017).

Content scope should be organized hierarchically, starting with major modules or units and breaking down into specific topics, subtopics, and learning activities. This structure supports the later development of work breakdown structures and project timelines (Project Management Institute, 2021).

Deliverables and Specifications

The scope statement should include a comprehensive list of all project deliverables, including both interim deliverables (such as design documents, prototypes, and review materials) and final deliverables (such as completed course modules, assessment materials, and implementation guides). Each deliverable should be accompanied by specific quality criteria and acceptance standards (Dick et al., 2015).

For online course development, deliverables might include course modules, interactive activities, assessment instruments, instructor guides, technical documentation, and quality assurance reports. The scope statement should specify the format, technical requirements, and standards for each deliverable (Morrison et al., 2019).

Technology and Platform Requirements

Modern instructional design projects must clearly define the technology infrastructure and platform requirements that will support the learning experience. This includes learning management system specifications, authoring tool requirements, multimedia standards, accessibility compliance requirements, and any integration needs with existing systems (Clark & Mayer, 2016).

Technology requirements should consider both current capabilities and future scalability needs. They should also address compatibility requirements across different devices and browsers, as well as bandwidth considerations for diverse learner populations (Gagné et al., 2005).

Project Constraints and Assumptions

Every project operates within certain constraints that limit options and influence decisions. The primary project constraints in Instructional Design include scope, time, and budget (eLearning Industry, 2023). The scope statement should clearly identify these constraints and explain how they will impact project decisions and deliverables.

Common constraints in instructional design projects include budget limitations, time restrictions, technology limitations, resource availability, and organizational policies. The scope statement should also document key assumptions about stakeholder availability, content accuracy, and external dependencies (Kerzner, 2017).
