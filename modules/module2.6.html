Module 2.6: Project Management for Instructional Design

Best Practices and Common Pitfalls

Best Practices for Scope Development

Successful scope development requires early and continuous stakeholder engagement to ensure that all requirements are identified and understood. The scope statement should be developed collaboratively with key stakeholders and subject matter experts to ensure buy-in and accuracy (Kerzner, 2017).

The scope statement should be written in clear, specific language that avoids ambiguity and misinterpretation. It should also be regularly reviewed and updated as the project progresses and new information becomes available (Allen & Gardner, 2014).

Common Timeline Development Pitfalls

Common pitfalls in timeline development include underestimating task complexity, failing to account for stakeholder availability, inadequate buffer time, and unrealistic assumptions about resource availability. These issues can be mitigated through careful planning, stakeholder communication, and regular timeline reviews (Greer & Lin, 2021).

Another common pitfall is the tendency to compress timelines under pressure from stakeholders without adequately assessing the impact on quality or feasibility. It's important to maintain realistic expectations and communicate the consequences of timeline compression clearly to all stakeholders (Project Management Institute, 2021).
