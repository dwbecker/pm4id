Module 3.1: Managing Faculty Relationships

Module 3: Overview

Module Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this module, you will be able to:

  • Identify key strategies for building effective collaborative relationships with faculty
  • Analyze communication best practices that foster trust and mutual respect
  • Develop approaches for managing conflicts and navigating challenging faculty dynamics
  • Create frameworks for establishing clear roles and expectations in collaborative projects
  • Implement techniques for maintaining long-term professional relationships with faculty partners

Module Summary

The relationship between instructional designers and faculty is fundamental to creating successful learning experiences. While course development often begins with a focus on course content, the collaborative relationship between faculty and instructional designers is secondary to the design process, yet these relationships matter for successful collaborative work (Oregon State University, 2022). Research indicates that when instructional designers and faculty work together effectively, they create more student-centered learning environments that directly impact student success.

This module explores evidence-based strategies for building and maintaining productive working relationships with faculty across diverse academic disciplines and institutional contexts.
