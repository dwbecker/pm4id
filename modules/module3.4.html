Module 3.4: Managing Faculty Relationships

Building Trust and Rapport

The Foundation of Trust

Trust forms the cornerstone of effective faculty-instructional designer relationships. Research has investigated perceptions of collaborative relationships between instructional designers and faculty, noting that little attention has been paid to what constitutes an effective collaboration and how it can be developed from the perspectives of both instructional designers and faculty (Educational Technology Research and Development, 2019).

Strategies for Building Trust

Demonstrating Competence: Show expertise in instructional design principles, learning technologies, and pedagogical approaches while respecting faculty content expertise.

Reliability: Consistently follow through on commitments, meet deadlines, and maintain professional standards.

Transparency: Be open about processes, challenges, and limitations. Share the reasoning behind design decisions.

Respect for Expertise: Acknowledge and value faculty knowledge while contributing complementary skills.

Overcoming Initial Skepticism

Some faculty may initially be skeptical about working with instructional designers. Strategies for overcoming this include:

  • Sharing success stories and examples of improved student outcomes.
  • Providing evidence-based rationales for design recommendations.
  • Starting with small, low-risk projects to demonstrate value.
  • Soliciting faculty input and incorporating their feedback meaningfully.
