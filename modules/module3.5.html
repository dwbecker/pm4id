Module 3.5: Managing Faculty Relationships

Collaborative Project Management

Defining Roles and Responsibilities

The easiest way of defining roles in the online course development process is to view the faculty member as a subject-matter expert and the instructional designer as a learning technology expert, with the faculty member primarily responsible for course content and assignments, and the instructional designer primarily responsible for how students interact with and consume that content in an online setting (Inside Higher Ed, 2018).

Project Workflow Management

Phase 1: Planning and Discovery

  • Conduct needs assessment interviews.
  • Establish project goals and constraints.
  • Create project timeline and milestones.
  • Identify potential challenges and mitigation strategies.

Phase 2: Design and Development

  • Maintain regular communication throughout the development process.
  • Provide multiple opportunities for faculty review and feedback.
  • Document decisions and rationale for future reference.
  • Adjust timelines as needed while maintaining quality standards.

Phase 3: Implementation and Evaluation

  • Support faculty during course launch.
  • Collect and analyze student feedback.
  • Conduct post-project evaluation with faculty.
  • Plan for future iterations and improvements.

Collaborative Decision-Making

Effective collaboration requires shared decision-making processes that respect both instructional design expertise and faculty knowledge. This involves:

  • Consensus Building: Working toward mutually acceptable solutions.
  • Compromise: Finding middle ground when perspectives differ.
  • Evidence-Based Decisions: Using data and research to inform choices.
  • Iterative Refinement: Continuously improving based on feedback and results.
