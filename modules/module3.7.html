Module 3.7: Managing Faculty Relationships

Long-Term Relationship Maintenance

Building Ongoing Partnerships

Successful instructional designers cultivate long-term relationships with faculty that extend beyond individual projects. Maintaining some form of regular communication between projects, possibly by sharing relevant research, resources, and professional development opportunities, helps faculty feel supported by someone who knows them. This sense of mutual support tends to lead faculty to request that they work with the same designers they have worked with on past projects. Acknowledging faculty contributions and successes also goes a long way in developing sustained relationships.

Continuous Improvement

Gathering, sharing, and discussing feedback in an honest and constructive manner should lead to refinements in the design process. This also enables all stakeholers to stay current with both instructional design and faculty needs, ideally by connecting faculty with other relevant professionals and resources available to them.

Measuring Relationship Success

There are several common indicators that you have developed successful relationships with faculty. The most common being faculty repeatedly requesting to work with the same instructional designer or referring other colleages to a specific designer. Favorable evaluations and testimonials is another positive indicator, and potentially helpful in convincing colleagues to take advantage of an instructional design shop. Other, more subtle and difficult to measure, indicators include improved student learning and engagement. Finally, we love to see faculty become more willing to try new teaching approaches or technologies in their online courses.
